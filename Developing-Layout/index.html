Favourite Places

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a world-renowned monument located in Agra, India, built between 1631 and 1653 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983, the Taj Mahal is celebrated as a symbol of eternal love and artistic excellence, attracting millions of visitors each year and standing as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Golder Temple

Founded by Guru Arjan in 1581 and completed in 1604 with the installation of the Adi Granth (the Sikh holy scripture), it stands as a symbol of spiritual devotion and equality. Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a magnificent royal residence located in Mysuru, Karnataka, India. It was originally the official seat of the Wadiyar dynasty, rulers of the Kingdom of Mysore. The current structure was commissioned in 1897 after a fire destroyed the old palace, and it was completed in 1912 under the design of British architect Henry Irwin. Built in the Indo-Saracenic style, the palace blends Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic architectural elements.

Varanasi Temple

The Varanasi Temples are among the most sacred and historic religious sites in India, located in the ancient city of Varanasi (also called Kashi or Banaras) on the banks of the River Ganga. The most famous is the Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, which is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas (the holiest Shiva shrines). This temple has been a center of Hindu devotion for centuries, symbolizing spiritual liberation and divine energy. Varanasi itself is often described as the spiritual capital of India, with thousands of temples, shrines, and ghats lining the river, where pilgrims perform rituals, prayers, and cremation ceremonies.
